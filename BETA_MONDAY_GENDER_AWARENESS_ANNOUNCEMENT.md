## üî¨ What MAIA Detects (From Behavior)

When enabled, MAIA observes:

1. **Communication Style**
   - Relational (contextual, connection-focused) vs. Analytical (problem-solving, logical)
   - Research: Tannen (1990), Baron-Cohen (2005)

2. **Emotional Disclosure**
   - Immediate vs. Gradual vs. Guarded
   - Research: Gilligan (1982)

3. **Integration Style**
   - Verbal (thinking it through) vs. Embodied (feeling in body) vs. Systemic (seeing patterns)
   - Research: Belenky et al. (1986)

4. **Stress Response**
   - Tend-and-befriend (seek connection) vs. Fight-flight (take action/escape) vs. Freeze (shut down)
   - Research: Taylor et al. (2000)

**All adaptations cite peer-reviewed neuroscience and psychology studies.**

## ‚ùì FAQ

**Q: Is this stereotyping?**
A: No. Your actual behavior matters more than any assumption. If observed patterns contradict your profile, we use the observed patterns (and track this to prove it).

**Q: What if I'm non-binary?**
A: Fully supported. The system doesn't force binary patterns. It detects YOUR communication style, whatever that is.

**Q: What if I don't want this?**
A: Don't enable it! It's 100% opt-in. System works exactly the same without it.

**Q: Can I change my mind later?**
A: Yes. Disable in settings anytime.

**Q: What research is this based on?**
A: See [full research foundation here](/docs/gender-aware-research). Every adaptation cites peer-reviewed studies.

**Q: Does this work for trans people?**
A: Yes. The system respects your stated identity and adapts to YOUR actual patterns, not assumptions.

**Q: Is this just for women?**
A: No. The research covers all gender identities, including masculine patterns, non-binary experiences, and individual variance.

**Q: What's the hormonal cycle tracking?**
A: Optional awareness that menstrual cycles, testosterone cycles, or stress-cortisol cycles affect emotional processing. Respects that ALL people have hormonal rhythms.
