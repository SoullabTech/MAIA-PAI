## How to Use This Commons

### For Practitioners
- **Browse**: Case studies, clinical protocols, session transcripts

- **Access**: Research protocols, measures, analysis templates
- **Share**: Your findings, datasets (anonymized), publications
- **Collaborate**: Find co-authors, research partners
- **Replicate**: Use standardized protocols for multi-site studies

### For Students
- **Study**: Educational materials, reading lists, video tutorials
- **Practice**: Sample assessments, practice transcripts
- **Ask**: Student forums, mentorship matching
- **Grow**: Track your learning journey

### For Developers
- **Build**: MAIA integrations, tools, plugins
- **Share**: Code repositories, technical documentation
- **Collaborate**: Open source contributions
- **Innovate**: New features, visualizations, analytics

## Contribution Guidelines

### General Principles

**1. Anonymity**: All client/patient information MUST be thoroughly anonymized
- Change names, locations, identifying details
- Create composites when possible
- Follow HIPAA/local privacy laws

**2. Attribution**: Credit sources appropriately
- Cite original Spiralogic framework (Soullab Collective, 2025)
- Acknowledge co-contributors
- Use Creative Commons licenses

**3. Quality**: Maintain high standards
- Clear writing
- Evidence-based claims
- Peer review before major contributions

**4. Respect**: Honor diverse perspectives
- Cultural sensitivity
- Theoretical pluralism
- Constructive critique

**5. Gift Economy**: What you receive, give back enriched
- Share learnings openly
- Support others' work
- Build on, don't duplicate

### How to Contribute

**Step 1: Choose Category**
- Case study? â†’ `/case-library/`

- Include all required sections

**Step 3: Submit**
- Via GitHub pull request (preferred)
- Via email to commons moderators
- Via community forum post

**Step 4: Peer Review**
- Community members review
- Feedback provided
- Revisions as needed

**Step 5: Publication**
- Approved contributions added to commons
- Contributor credited
- Shared with community

## Community Protocols

### Code of Conduct

**We are committed to**:
- Respect for all participants (regardless of background, identity, theoretical orientation)
- Confidentiality (clinical material stays anonymous)
- Integrity (honest reporting, ethical practice)
- Collaboration (sharing knowledge, supporting growth)
- Accountability (taking responsibility for contributions)

**We do not tolerate**:
- Harassment or discrimination
- Breach of confidentiality
- Plagiarism or misattribution
- Harmful advice or unethical practices
- Commercial spam or self-promotion (without disclosure)

**Violations**: Report to moderators@soullab.org (to be created)

### Ethics Guidelines

**For Clinical Contributions**:
- [ ] Client consent obtained (if identifiable in any way)
- [ ] All identifying information removed or altered
- [ ] Clinical work within scope of practice
- [ ] Safety prioritized over theoretical purity
- [ ] Uncertainty acknowledged when present

**For Research Contributions**:
- [ ] IRB approval obtained (if human subjects)
- [ ] Data properly anonymized
- [ ] Methods transparently reported
- [ ] Limitations acknowledged
- [ ] Conflicts of interest disclosed

**For Educational Contributions**:
- [ ] Accurate representation of framework
- [ ] Level-appropriate content (awareness adaptation)
- [ ] Sources properly cited
- [ ] Safety warnings included when needed

### Peer Review Process

**For Case Studies**:
1. **Initial Review** (Moderator): Anonymity check, formatting
2. **Clinical Review** (2 practitioners): Clinical soundness, safety
3. **Theoretical Review** (1 researcher): Alignment with framework
4. **Approval**: Published to commons

**For Research**:
1. **Initial Review** (Moderator): Ethics compliance, formatting
2. **Methodological Review** (2 researchers): Methods, analysis
3. **Theoretical Review** (1 expert): Framework interpretation
4. **Approval**: Published to commons

**For Protocols**:
1. **Initial Review** (Moderator): Safety check, formatting
2. **Clinical Review** (3 practitioners): Feasibility, safety, effectiveness
3. **Pilot Testing** (Optional): Small group tests protocol
4. **Approval**: Published as "Community Protocol" (not official)

**Timeline**: Typical review is 2-4 weeks

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to be certified to contribute?**
A: No formal certification required, but contributors should have appropriate credentials (licensed therapist, graduate student, etc.) and familiarity with Spiralogic framework.

**Q: Can I use commons resources in my practice?**
A: Yes! All resources are Creative Commons licensed. Use freely with attribution.

**Q: How do I ensure client confidentiality when submitting cases?**
A: Use our anonymization checklist. When in doubt, create composites or disguise more details. Moderators will review before publication.

**Q: Can I submit work that critiques or diverges from Spiralogic?**
A: Absolutely. Constructive critique and alternative perspectives are welcome. This commons values intellectual honesty.

**Q: Is there a cost to access commons resources?**
A: No. The commons is free and open. Consider contributing if you benefit.

**Q: How do I report unethical content or behavior?**
A: Email moderators@soullab.org (to be created) with details. All reports confidential.

**Q: Can I use commons resources for commercial purposes (courses, books)?**
A: Individual resources vary (check license). Generally, attribution and share-alike required. Large commercial use may require permission.

**Q: How can I financially support the commons?**
A: Donate to Soullab fiscal sponsorship (details coming). All funds support commons infrastructure, moderation, and community development.
