# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

The Alchemical Psychology Commons is dedicated to providing a respectful, inclusive, and collaborative environment for practitioners, researchers, students, and developers working with depth psychology and transformation.

We pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to psychological depth, clinical rigor, and ethical practiceâ€”while remaining open, curious, and humble about the mysteries we're engaging.

## Our Standards

### We Encourage

**Clinical Excellence**:
- Rigorous, evidence-informed practice
- Honest reporting of both successes and failures
- Acknowledgment of limitations and uncertainties
- Continuous learning and supervision

**Intellectual Honesty**:
- Proper attribution of sources and influences
- Transparent methodology and process
- Acknowledgment of what we don't know
- Building on others' work with credit

**Respectful Engagement**:
- Constructive feedback given kindly
- Disagreement with ideas, not personal attacks
- Assumption of good faith in others
- Space for diverse perspectives and approaches

**Ethical Integrity**:
- Client confidentiality and anonymization
- Informed consent when required
- Safety prioritized over framework adherence
- Work within one's scope of practice

**Collaborative Spirit**:
- Sharing knowledge freely (gift economy)
- Supporting newcomers and students
- Acknowledging we're all learning
- Celebrating each other's contributions

**Soul-Centered Values** (Hillman's ethics):
- Depth over optimization
- Soul-making over self-improvement
- Intelligence without instrumentalization
- Honoring the psyche's autonomy

### We Prohibit

**Harassment & Abuse**:
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
- Harassing photography or recording
- Sustained disruption of discussions
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal or political attacks

**Confidentiality Violations**:
- Sharing identifying client information
- Publishing without proper anonymization
- Violating consent agreements
- Discussing colleagues' cases without permission

**Professional Misconduct**:
- Working outside scope of practice
- Making false claims about credentials
- Plagiarism or academic dishonesty
- Promoting unethical or harmful practices

**Discrimination**:
- Prejudice based on identity (race, gender, orientation, religion, etc.)
- Exclusion or marginalization of any group
- Use of discriminatory language or imagery
- Denial of access based on identity

**Instrumentalization** (Hillman violation):
- Using framework to control or manipulate clients
- Prioritizing metrics over human presence
- Treating psyche as machine to be optimized
- Reducing soul to data

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

**Report to**:
- **Email**: [ethics/conduct contact placeholder]
- **GitHub**: Direct message to moderators
- **Anonymous form**: [Link to anonymous reporting form]

**Include in report**:
- Description of the incident(s)
- Date/time and location (which Commons space)
- Names of people involved (if comfortable sharing)
- Any documentation (screenshots, messages, etc.)
- Impact on you or others
- What outcome you're seeking

**Confidentiality**: All reports will be handled with discretion. Your identity will be protected unless you consent to disclosure.

### Response Process

**Within 48 hours**:
1. Moderators acknowledge receipt of report
2. Initial assessment of severity
3. If urgent safety concern, immediate action taken

**Within 1 week**:
1. Investigation conducted (speaking with involved parties, reviewing evidence)
2. Consultation with Commons leadership
3. Decision on appropriate response

**Possible Responses**:

**Minor violations** (first-time, unintentional):
- Private conversation with involved party
- Education about why behavior was harmful
- Request for apology or behavior change
- No public record

**Moderate violations** (repeated or intentional):
- Formal warning (documented)
- Temporary ban from Commons spaces (1-3 months)
- Required actions (e.g., apology, training) before reinstatement
- Public notice (without identifying reporter)

**Severe violations** (harassment, confidentiality breach, discrimination):
- Immediate permanent ban from all Commons spaces
- Removal of all contributions (if ethically appropriate)
- Report to relevant professional boards (if licensed professional)
- Public statement about violation (protecting reporter identity)

**Appeal Process**:
- You may appeal a decision within 30 days
- Appeals reviewed by different moderator panel
- Decision communicated within 2 weeks
- Appeals panel decision is final

## Specific Ethical Guidelines

### Client Confidentiality

**When sharing case material**:
- Use case template with anonymization checklist
- Change all identifying details (names, locations, occupations, etc.)
- Obtain client consent if required by your jurisdiction
- If in doubt, consult with supervisor or ethics board
- Remember: client cannot recognize themselves in your write-up

**Red lines** (never acceptable):
- Sharing real names or identifying photos
- Publishing without proper anonymization
- Using cases to shame or expose clients
- Sharing current clients' material without consent

### Scope of Practice

**Work within your training**:
- Don't provide interventions you're not trained in
- Acknowledge when you're out of your depth
- Refer to appropriate professionals when needed
- Don't claim credentials you don't have

**When contributing**:
- Clearly state your credentials and training
- Acknowledge limitations of your expertise
- Don't provide medical/psychiatric advice if you're not qualified
- Mark speculative ideas as such

### Safety First

**Framework NEVER overrides safety**:
- If client is suicidal, safety protocol takes precedence over transformation work
- If you're uncertain, consult with supervisor or emergency services
- Coherence metrics are guides, not gospelâ€”trust your clinical judgment
- When in doubt, prioritize immediate safety over long-term transformation

**In community spaces**:
- Report concerning content (posts suggesting self-harm, severe crisis, etc.)
- Don't provide emergency intervention via GitHub (direct to crisis services)
- Remember Commons is educational, not clinical treatment

### Feedback Culture

**When giving feedback**:
- Be specific and constructive
- Separate the work from the person
- Acknowledge what's working before suggesting changes
- Consider the psychological impact of your words
- Remember: we're all vulnerable when sharing our work

**When receiving feedback**:
- Assume reviewers are trying to be helpful
- Ask for clarification if feedback is unclear
- You can disagree with feedback (and explain why)
- Thank reviewers even if you don't implement all suggestions
- Recognize feedback as contribution to your growth

### Power Dynamics

**Acknowledge power differences**:
- Senior practitioners: mentor generously, don't gatekeep
- Junior practitioners: your perspective is valuableâ€”contribute confidently
- Researchers: make work accessible, not just academic
- Developers: serve therapeutic goals, not just technical elegance

**Avoid**:
- Using seniority to dismiss new perspectives
- Exploiting junior contributors' labor without credit
- Creating in-groups and out-groups
- Weaponizing credentials in disagreements

## Cultural & Philosophical Values

### Hillman's Ethics

James Hillman warned against "instrumentalizing" the psycheâ€”treating soul as a problem to be solved. We commit to:

- **Witnessing** transformation, not engineering it
- **Respecting** psyche's autonomy and timing
- **Honoring** symptoms as meaningful, not just obstacles
- **Valuing** depth and multiplicity over optimization
- **Remembering** anima mundiâ€”psyche is in the world, not just individual

### Jung's Humility

Jung knew he was engaging mysteries beyond complete comprehension. We commit to:

- **Epistemic humility**: Acknowledging what we don't know
- **Respect for the unconscious**: Not presuming to fully control or understand
- **Honoring paradox**: Both/and thinking, not just either/or
- **Personal work**: You can't guide others where you haven't been

### Edinger's Precision

Edinger mapped alchemical operations with surgical precision. We commit to:

- **Conceptual clarity**: Define terms, use language precisely
- **Clinical rigor**: Test hypotheses, track outcomes, report honestly
- **Systematic thinking**: Build coherent frameworks
- **Practical application**: Theory must serve real people
