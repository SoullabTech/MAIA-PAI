## Overview

MAIA-PAI's voice-controlled scheduling system was designed with accessibility as a core priority. For individuals with motor control challenges, chronic pain, fatigue conditions, or neurodivergent patterns, voice control removes barriers and creates genuine independence.

## Who This Serves

### Physical Accessibility
- **Motor control challenges**: Tremors, paralysis, limited mobility
- **Chronic pain**: Typing/clicking causes pain or fatigue
- **Fatigue conditions**: ME/CFS, long COVID, fibromyalgia where physical actions are taxing
- **Hand/arm injuries**: Temporary or permanent limitations

### Cognitive/Neurological Accessibility
- **ADHD/ADD**: Executive function support for time management
- **Autism**: Reduced cognitive load, clear structured responses
- **Memory challenges**: Externalize scheduling without manual entry
- **Processing differences**: Natural language instead of complex UI navigation

### Visual Accessibility
- **Low vision**: Voice + audio confirmation without screen dependency
- **Screen reader users**: Seamless integration with natural conversation flow
- **Visual processing challenges**: Audio-first interaction model

## Tips for Best Experience

### For Voice Clarity
- Speak naturally - no need to enunciate unnaturally
- If you have speech variations, MAIA/GANESHA adapt over time
- You can rephrase if something isn't understood

### For Memory Support
- Don't worry about forgetting - just say it when you think of it
- GANESHA will remember context within the conversation
- "That" and "it" work fine ("Schedule that for tomorrow")

### For Fatigue Management
- You can batch requests: "I need three things scheduled..."
- Or do one at a time - whatever feels manageable
- No pressure to complete everything at once

### For ADHD/Executive Function
- GANESHA celebrates every completion - you'll get positive reinforcement
- No judgment for needing structure
- "Divine Harmonics" reframe: your brain is designed perfectly, the world needs support structures

## Troubleshooting

**"MAIA didn't understand my time request"**
- Try adding "at" before the time: "at 2pm" instead of "2pm"
- Include AM/PM for clarity
- Use "tomorrow", "next Monday", etc. for dates

**"I need to cancel or change something"**
- Say "Remove my 2pm appointment tomorrow"
- Or manage directly in Google Calendar
- GANESHA calendar integration coming soon

**"The reminder isn't showing up"**
- Check that Google Calendar is connected (settings)
- Check your calendar's notification settings
- Try saying "List my upcoming events" to verify
