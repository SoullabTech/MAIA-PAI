# Proposal Customization Guide
## How to Transform the Template into a Personal, Winning Proposal

**Time Required:** 2-3 hours per proposal
**Success Rate:** Well-customized proposals convert at 40-60% vs. 10-20% for generic ones

---

## The Golden Rule

**Every client should feel like this proposal was written specifically for THEM, not adapted from a template.**

Signs of good customization:
‚úÖ Their exact words appear in the proposal
‚úÖ Specific details from their questionnaire are referenced
‚úÖ Features are prioritized based on THEIR goals
‚úÖ Pricing matches their budget range
‚úÖ Timeline aligns with their launch date

Signs of poor customization:
‚ùå Generic language ("your business", "your customers")
‚ùå Features they didn't ask for
‚ùå Budget way outside their range
‚ùå Copy-paste feel

## Red Flags (When to Adjust Proposal)

### Budget Mismatch
**Problem:** They want Thrive features ($30-50k) but have Seed budget ($8-12k)

**Solution:**
> "Based on your goals and budget, I want to be transparent: building everything you want for $10k isn't possible without compromising quality. Here are three paths forward:
>
> 1. **MVP Now, Grow Later** - Launch with core features for $12k, add advanced features as revenue grows
> 2. **Extend Timeline** - Spread payments over 12 months instead of 3 to make it cashflow-friendly
> 3. **Adjust Scope** - Prioritize your top 3 features, table the rest for Phase 2
>
> Which approach feels best for In Home Sanctuary?"

### Unrealistic Timeline
**Problem:** They want to launch in 6 weeks (you need 12-16)

**Solution:**
> "I want to honor your urgency AND build something that lasts. Rushing results in technical debt and a janky experience. Here's what I can do:
>
> - **Quick Win** - Launch a simple landing page + pre-orders in 2 weeks (capture demand now)
> - **Phased Launch** - Core features in 8 weeks (faster than normal), advanced features Month 3
> - **Be Honest** - If September 1 is critical for business reasons (seasonal sales), let's discuss if a simpler solution bridges you until we build the full version"

### Unclear Vision
**Problem:** Their questionnaire is vague or contradictory

**Solution:**
Don't send proposal yet. Instead:
> "Kristen, I want to make sure I'm proposing the right solution. A few things from your questionnaire weren't clear to me. Can we hop on a 30-minute call to clarify:
>
> - [Specific question 1]
> - [Specific question 2]
> - [Specific question 3]
>
> This will help me create a proposal that actually fits‚Äînot a generic pitch."

### Proposal Review Call Script

**Agenda (60 min):**
1. (5 min) How are you feeling about it?
2. (25 min) Walk through proposal section by section
3. (20 min) Address questions and concerns
4. (10 min) Discuss next steps and timeline

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- "What excited you most?"
- "What gave you pause?"
- "Is the budget in range or do we need to adjust?"
- "Is the timeline realistic for you?"
- "Imagining this live in 4 months‚Äîwhat feels missing?"

**Objection Handling:**

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "It's more than I budgeted" | "I get it. Let's talk about what's essential vs. nice-to-have. Where can we trim?" |
| "I need to think about it" | "Absolutely. What specifically do you need to think through? Maybe I can help." |
| "I'm not sure about AI" | "Would it help to see a demo? I can show you MAIA in action with another client." |
| "My partner needs to approve" | "Of course. Would it help if I joined a call with both of you to answer questions?" |
