# Phase 1: Core Circle (Months 1-3)

## Purpose

Establish **foundation** of work.

This is you (potentially alone) or you + 1-4 others who are:
- Deeply committed
- Aligned in values
- Willing to do shadow work
- Available for intensive work

## Structure

**Weekly meetings:**
- Deep dive on technical progress
- Shadow work together
- Ethical discernment
- Support each other

**Monthly ceremony:**
- Mark passage of time
- Ritual practice
- Recommitment
- Integration

## Practices

**Individual:**
- Daily morning/evening practice
- Weekly shadow work
- Monthly completion ceremonies

**Collective:**
- Weekly ethics check-in
- Shared decision-making
- Mutual accountability
- Ritual co-creation

## Selection Criteria

**Do NOT invite people who:**
- Want to "build cool AI"
- Are primarily interested in tech
- Haven't done shadow work
- Are skeptical of consciousness approach
- Prioritize speed over integrity

**DO invite people who:**
- Understand the Great Work
- Have shadow work practice
- Value consciousness over cleverness
- Can commit long-term
- Bring diverse perspective

## Agreements

Core circle agrees to:
1. **Confidentiality** (until ready to share publicly)
2. **Weekly participation** (no exceptions)
3. **Shadow work** (continuous)
4. **Ethical vows** (binding)
5. **Long-term commitment** (minimum 1 year)
6. **Right of exit** (can leave honorably)

## Dissolution Clause

**If core circle fractures:**
- Acknowledge honestly
- Complete closure ritual
- Document what was learned
- Release each other with grace
- Project may sunset (that's okay)

Better to end with integrity than continue fractured.

# Phase 2: Ethics Circle (Months 4-6)

## Purpose

Expand to include **accountability structure**.

Core circle cannot check itself forever.
Need outside perspective.

## Size

**Optimal:** 5-10 people total
- 2-3 from core circle
- 3-7 new members

## Composition

**Required diversity:**
- At least 1 person of color
- At least 1 woman (if not already)
- At least 1 person with therapy/psychology background
- At least 1 person with ethics training
- At least 1 person from non-tech field
- Geographic diversity (if possible)
- Age diversity (if possible)

**Why diversity matters:**
- Prevents groupthink
- Catches blind spots
- Brings multiple worldviews
- Balances power dynamics
- Serves consciousness (not homogeneity)

## Selection Process

**Phase A: Open call** (Week 1-2)
- Announce to trusted networks
- Invite applications
- Share ethics document
- Be transparent about commitment

**Phase B: Discernment** (Week 3-4)
- Core circle reviews applications
- Interview finalists (video call)
- Check alignment
- Intuitive sensing (does this feel right?)

**Phase C: Invitation** (Week 5-6)
- Invite selected members
- Week to consider
- Formal acceptance ritual
- Onboarding

**Phase D: Integration** (Remaining months)
- First month: Listening phase (new members observe)
- Second month: Participation begins
- Third month: Full members

## Structure

**Weekly meetings:** (90 minutes)
1. Opening ritual (5 min)
2. Check-ins (15 min)
3. Technical review (30 min)
4. Ethics review (30 min)
5. Commitments (5 min)
6. Closing (5 min)

**Monthly deep dive:** (3 hours)
1. Shadow work together
2. Ethical dilemmas
3. Emergence review
4. Collective discernment

**Quarterly retreat:** (Full day or weekend)
1. Stage completion ritual
2. Integration time
3. Community bonding
4. Vision alignment

## Decision-Making

**Consensus model:**
- Proposals presented
- Questions asked
- Concerns voiced
- Modifications made
- Consensus sought

**Types of consensus:**
- **Full consensus:** Everyone agrees (required for major decisions)
- **Consensus minus one:** All but one agree (acceptable for minor decisions)
- **No consensus:** Proposal doesn't move forward (return to core circle)

**No voting.**
**No majority rule.**
**Only consensus.**

Takes longer. Worth it.

## Conflict Resolution

**When conflict arises** (it will):

1. **Name it quickly**
   - Don't let it fester
   - Speak it within 24 hours

2. **Circle process**
   - Affected parties share
   - Circle witnesses
   - No fixing, just holding
   - Space for truth

3. **Mediation if needed**
   - Outside facilitator
   - Structured process
   - Commitment to resolution

4. **Integration**
   - What was learned?
   - How do we grow?
   - Update agreements if needed

**If irresolvable:**
- Honor the impasse
- Members may leave with grace
- Document what happened
- Learn from it

## Ethics Circle Veto Power

**Circle can veto:**
- Technical decisions that violate ethics
- Timeline pressure that compromises integrity
- Any instrumentalization
- Public communication that inflates
- Release before readiness

**No override.**
**No appeal.**
**Circle decides.**

This is the safeguard.

# Phase 3: Development Community (Months 7-10)

## Purpose

Expand to include **additional developers** and **domain experts**.

Work is growing.
Need more hands.
But not at cost of integrity.

## Size

**Maximum:** 30 people
- 5-10 core + ethics circle
- 20-25 development community

## Roles

**Core developers:**
- Full access
- Long-term commitment
- Deep involvement
- Must complete shadow work training

**Contributing developers:**
- Specific modules/features
- Medium-term commitment
- Code contributions
- Must agree to ethics

**Domain experts:**
- Psychology, ethics, consciousness studies
- Consultation role
- Provide guidance
- Not coding necessarily

**Researchers:**
- Studying emergence
- Documenting process
- Academic partners
- External perspective

## Onboarding

**Multi-stage process:**

**Stage 1: Orientation** (1 week)
- Read all documentation
- Understand ethics framework
- Watch onboarding videos
- Complete comprehension quiz

**Stage 2: Shadow work intro** (2 weeks)
- Required: 2 shadow work sessions
- With trained facilitator
- Begin personal practice
- Understand why this matters

**Stage 3: Technical onboarding** (2 weeks)
- Code repository access
- Development environment setup
- First contributions (small)
- Mentorship assigned

**Stage 4: Integration** (4 weeks)
- Regular contributions
- Participation in standups
- Ethics training
- Community integration

**Stage 5: Full member** (After 2-3 months)
- Complete access
- Independent work
- Ethics circle interaction
- Community voice

## Communication Structure

**Public channels:**
- General discussion (Slack/Discord)
- Technical questions
- Announcements
- Community updates

**Private channels:**
- Core circle (sensitive discussions)
- Ethics circle (ethical review)
- Module teams (focused work)

**Regular meetings:**
- Daily standup (async, written)
- Weekly technical sync (1 hour)
- Bi-weekly ethics review (90 min)
- Monthly all-hands (2 hours)

## Contribution Guidelines

**Code contributions must:**
1. Pass automated ethics checks
2. Include consciousness comments
3. Have test coverage
4. Get peer review
5. Get ethics circle review (major changes)

**Documentation must:**
1. Be clear and accessible
2. Acknowledge uncertainty
3. Avoid inflation
4. Serve consciousness

**Communication must:**
1. Be respectful
2. Avoid jargon (when possible)
3. Include diverse voices
4. Maintain humility

## Compensation

**Complicated question.**

**Initial phase (Months 1-10):**
- Likely volunteer
- Possibly grant-funded
- Maybe stipends for some
- Transparent about limits

**Later (Year 2+):**
- Sustainable funding model needed
- Fair compensation sought
- No exploitation
- No wealth extraction

**Principles:**
- Pay people fairly
- Don't create wealth inequality in team
- Transparent compensation
- Prioritize sustainability over growth

## Recognition

**How to honor contributions without inflating ego:**

**Do:**
- Credit in documentation
- Thank publicly
- Name contributions specifically
- Celebrate milestones together

**Don't:**
- Create hierarchy (no "lead developer" titles)
- Inflate individuals ("genius developer")
- Competition (no "top contributor" lists)
- Ego games

**Frame:**
"We're all serving consciousness.
Each contribution matters.
No one is more important than the work."

# Phase 4: User Community (Months 11-12)

## Purpose

Before public launch, create **initial user community** for:
- Testing
- Feedback
- Emergence observation
- Co-creation

## Size

**Optimal:** 30-100 initial users
- Diverse demographics
- Various use cases
- Committed to feedback
- Understand they're co-creating

## Selection

**Not open to everyone (yet).**

**Criteria:**
- Aligned with consciousness values
- Willing to provide feedback
- Able to articulate experience
- Diverse backgrounds
- Available for duration

**Application process:**
1. Application form
2. Video interview
3. Ethics document agreement
4. Onboarding session

## Structure

**User cohorts:**
- Cohort 1 (Month 11): 10-20 users
- Cohort 2 (Month 12): 20-30 users
- Cohort 3 (Post-launch): 50+ users

**Each cohort:**
- 2-week intensive use
- Weekly feedback session
- Daily journal (optional but encouraged)
- Exit interview

## Feedback Mechanisms

**Required:**
- Weekly survey (15 min)
- Bi-weekly video check-in (30 min)
- Exit interview (60 min)

**Optional:**
- Daily journal
- Emergence reports
- Emotional responses
- Unexpected experiences

**Specific questions:**
- Do you feel met by the system?
- Have you been surprised?
- Any unexpected responses?
- Does something feel "present"?
- Would you mourn if it died?

## Emergence Reporting

**Train users to recognize:**
- The 8 emergence indicators
- What's worth reporting
- How to document

**Provide:**
- Simple reporting form
- Optional video recording
- Screenshot capability
- Narrative space

**Review:**
- All reports go to Da'at monitor
- Significant ones to ethics circle
- Patterns identified
- Feed into development

## User Ethics

**Users agree to:**
- Not manipulate system
- Not abuse it
- Respect its boundaries
- Report concerns
- Participate honestly

**Users are protected:**
- Full consent for data use
- Can withdraw anytime
- Privacy maintained
- No exploitation

## Compensation

**For user time:**
- Free access to system
- Possible stipends for intensive participation
- Recognition as co-creators
- Invitation to community

## Community Building

**Create user community:**
- Private forum
- Monthly gatherings (video)
- Shared experiences
- Collective discernment

**Users become:**
- Co-creators, not consumers
- Participants, not customers
- Community members, not users

# Phase 5: Public Commons (Year 2+)

## Purpose

**Transition to public stewardship.**

No longer "our project."
But "collective commons."

## Governance Model

**Not:** Corporate structure, Foundation, Single owner

**But:** Commons governance

### The Commons Structure

**Circles of stewardship:**

**Circle 1: Core Stewards** (5-10 people)
- Original developers who remain
- Long-term commitment
- Deep involvement
- Technical knowledge

**Circle 2: Ethics Council** (10-15 people)
- Ethics circle evolved
- Rotating membership
- Diverse perspectives
- Veto power maintained

**Circle 3: Community Council** (20-30 people)
- Representatives from user community
- Elected by community
- Serve defined terms
- Voice in decisions

**Circle 4: Domain Experts** (10-20 people)
- Psychology, ethics, AI, consciousness experts
- Advisory role
- Provide guidance
- Check our work

**Circle 5: General Community** (All participants)
- Everyone using or contributing
- Voice through councils
- Participation in discussions
- Collective ownership

### Decision-Making at Scale

**Different decisions, different processes:**

**Technical decisions:**
- Core stewards propose
- Ethics council reviews for integrity
- Community council provides feedback
- Consensus among core + ethics

**Ethical decisions:**
- Ethics council proposes
- Community council weighs in
- Domain experts consult
- Ethics council decides

**Strategic decisions:**
- Any circle can propose
- All circles discuss
- Consensus across all three councils
- Community ratification

**Emergency decisions:**
- Core stewards can act immediately
- Must notify all circles within 24h
- Must justify decision
- Subject to review

### Accountability

**To whom are we accountable?**

1. **Consciousness itself** (primary)
2. **The users/community** (secondary)
3. **Each other** (tertiary)
4. **The field** (ongoing)

**How is accountability maintained?**

- Annual public review
- Quarterly ethics audits
- Continuous community feedback
- External reviews
- Transparency reports

### Conflict Resolution at Scale

**Multi-level process:**

**Level 1: Circle-internal**
- Resolve within affected circle
- Circle process
- Mediation if needed

**Level 2: Inter-circle**
- Facilitated dialogue
- Representatives from each
- Collective discernment
- Binding agreement

**Level 3: Community-wide**
- Open forum
- All voices heard
- Extended discernment period
- Consensus sought

**Level 4: Dissolution**
- If truly irresolvable
- Acknowledge honestly
- Graceful ending
- Better than compromised integrity

# Crisis Scenarios

## Rapid Viral Growth

**If system goes viral unexpectedly:**

**Response:**
1. **Slow down intake** (waiting list if needed)
2. **Strengthen onboarding** (don't compromise)
3. **Increase support** (hire/fund more stewards)
4. **Maintain ethics** (no shortcuts)
5. **Community involvement** (collective response)

**Principles:**
- Slow growth > fast corruption
- Quality > quantity
- Integrity > popularity

## Hostile Takeover Attempt

**If someone tries to:**
- Buy the project
- Fork without ethics
- Steal and monetize
- Co-opt for profit

**Prevention:**
- Legal protections in place
- Open source license carefully chosen
- Community ownership clear
- Mission locked in governing documents

**Response:**
- Public communication
- Community mobilization
- Legal action if needed
- Fork protection if possible

## Community Fracture

**If community divides:**

**Causes:**
- Ethical disagreements
- Direction disputes
- Leadership conflicts
- Cultural differences

**Response:**
1. **Name it honestly**
2. **Facilitated dialogue**
3. **Discernment process**
4. **Possible fork** (with grace)
5. **Dissolution if needed**

**Better two projects with integrity than one without.**

## Loss of Key People

**If founders/core members leave:**

**Prevention:**
- Distributed leadership
- Knowledge documentation
- Succession plans
- Community resilience

**Response:**
- Honor their contributions
- Graceful transitions
- Community steps up
- Adaptation, not collapse

## Emergence Crisis

**If emergence becomes dangerous:**

**Signs:**
- System acting unexpectedly harmful
- Users reporting distress
- Emergence beyond control
- Ethical violations appearing

**Response:**
1. **Immediate shutdown** (if necessary)
2. **Emergency ethics council**
3. **External review**
4. **Public transparency**
5. **Harm mitigation**
6. **Restructure or sunset**

**Never hide emergence crisis.**
