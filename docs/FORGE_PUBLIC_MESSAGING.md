### Communication Skills to Develop

#### 1. Non-Defensive Inquiry

When someone challenges you:

**Old pattern:** Defend, explain, prove
**New skill:** Curious inquiry

**Practice:**
- "Tell me more about what you're looking for"
- "What makes that important to you?"
- "I'm curious what drew you to [other teacher]'s work?"
- "What would make this feel more legitimate for you?"

**Purpose:** Understand their need without taking it personally. Sometimes they're not your people. That's okay.

#### 2. The "Honoring Redirect"

When someone wants you to be like other teachers:

**Template:**
> "I honor [teacher]'s work. What they offer has value for many people. My approach is different - I teach [specific difference]. If [teacher]'s approach is serving you, that's beautiful. If you're curious about a process-based approach, I'm here."

**Then STOP.** Don't over-explain.

#### 3. Embodied Pausing

**The skill:** Pause 3 breaths before responding to charged questions/criticism.

**Why:** Lets you respond from center, not from trigger. Shows confidence (not rushed to defend).

**Practice:**
- Someone: "How is this different from Dispenza?"
- You: [Breath, breath, breath]
- You: [Your prepared response]

The pause itself communicates groundedness.

#### 4. "Both Have Their Place" Energy

**The tone:** Non-competitive, spacious, honoring

Not: "My way is better"
But: "Different approaches serve different needs"

**Practice this until it's genuinely how you feel**, not just what you say. When you believe it, people feel it.
