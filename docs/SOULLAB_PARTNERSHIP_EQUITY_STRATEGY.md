---

### Option 2: Founder + Technical Co-Founder (PARTNERSHIP)

**Structure:**
- You: 70-80% (Founder, CEO, Vision)
- Nathan: 15-20% (CTO, Engineering, Systems)
- Reserved for employees: 5-10% (option pool)

**Vesting Schedule (CRITICAL):**
- Nathan's equity vests over 4 years
- 1-year cliff (he gets 0% if leaves before 1 year)
- After 1 year: 25% of his shares vest
- After that: Monthly vesting (6.25% every 3 months)
- If he leaves before 4 years, he only keeps what's vested

**Example:**
- Nathan gets 20% equity offer
- Year 1: He earns 5% (25% of 20%)
- Year 2: He earns another 5% (now has 10%)
- Year 3: He earns another 5% (now has 15%)
- Year 4: He earns final 5% (now has full 20%)

**If he leaves after 2 years:** He keeps 10%, you keep 10% back.

**Pros:**
- Nathan has real skin in the game
- Attracts committed partner
- Standard Silicon Valley structure
- Vesting protects you if he leaves early

**Cons:**
- Legal docs needed (expensive: $2k-5k)
- Harder to undo if relationship sours
- He can outvote you if you give him 30%+

**When This Works:**
- Nathan is ALL IN (quitting other work to build Soullab)
- You need serious engineering expertise full-time
- You trust him deeply
- You're planning to raise outside funding eventually

---

## Protecting Yourself: Legal Basics

### Documents You Need (With Nathan or Any Partner)

1. **Founders Agreement** ($1,000-2,000 from startup lawyer)
   - Who owns what %
   - Vesting schedule
   - Decision-making process
   - What happens if someone leaves
   - Intellectual property assignment

2. **Vesting Schedule** (included in Founders Agreement)
   - 4-year vest, 1-year cliff (standard)
   - Monthly vesting after cliff
   - Accelerated vesting triggers (if company sells, etc.)

3. **Operating Agreement** (if you form LLC)
   - How company operates
   - Voting rights
   - Profit distribution
   - Member responsibilities

4. **IP Assignment Agreement**
   - Anything Nathan builds for Soullab = Soullab owns it
   - Anything you built before = you own it
   - Critical to prevent disputes later

### Where to Get These

**Affordable options:**
- **Clerky.com**: $1,500 all-in for startup docs (recommended)
- **Stripe Atlas**: $500 + state fees (if incorporating)
- **Rocket Lawyer**: $400-800 (templates + lawyer review)

**Don't cheap out:**
- DON'T use free templates without lawyer review
- DON'T do handshake deals on equity
- DON'T assume "we'll figure it out later"

One fight with a partner over equity = $20k+ in legal fees. Pay $2k now to prevent that.
