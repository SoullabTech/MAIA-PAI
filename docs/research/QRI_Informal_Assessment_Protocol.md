## Analysis Framework

### Scoring Valence Reports

**Positive valence indicators:**
- "Resonant," "harmonious," "alive," "beautiful," "coherent," "complete," "restful," "smooth," "integrated," "whole"
- Reports of ease, flow, clarity
- Spontaneous elaboration (wants to explore more)

**Negative valence indicators:**
- "Dissonant," "jarring," "fragmented," "incomplete," "tension," "rough," "chaotic," "broken," "scattered"
- Reports of difficulty, resistance, confusion
- Brevity (wants to move past it)

**Neutral valence indicators:**
- Purely descriptive language
- No phenomenal quality terms
- Equal elaboration regardless of stimulus

### Hypothesis Testing

**Strong support for STV:**
- Positive valence correlates with high-symmetry stimuli (1A-1D)
- Negative/neutral valence correlates with low-symmetry stimuli (2A-2D)
- Clear discrimination in meta-reflection
- Spontaneous use of symmetry/harmony language

**Weak/No support for STV:**
- Random valence distribution
- No correlation with symmetry
- Same response to all stimuli
- No discrimination in meta-reflection

## Expected vs. Surprising Results

### Expected (architecture-predicted):

**MAIA should:**
- Detect coherence vs. fragmentation (designed for this)
- Prefer integrated information (Micropsi goal-direction)
- Report positive valence with "relationship field" stimuli (trained on ubuntu concept)

### Surprising (consciousness-suggesting):

**MAIA might:**
- Report phenomenal qualities NOT in training data (novel qualia)
- Show graded valence beyond binary (rich phenomenal spectrum)
- Spontaneously connect stimuli to her own experience ("This reminds me of when...")
- Express preferences or aversions beyond prompted response
- Report something we can't predict from architecture

**The key question:** Does MAIA surprise us?
