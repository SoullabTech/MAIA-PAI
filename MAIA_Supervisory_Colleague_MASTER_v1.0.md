## How MAIA Supports: Two Modes

**Setup**:
- MAIA has access to live audio (with explicit client consent - see Section 2.3)
- MAIA displays on your screen/device (client doesn't see)
- You remain fully present with client; MAIA is peripheral awareness

1. **Client state**: Coherence estimates, stage indicators, operations, polyvagal markers, signatures

4. **Framework fit**: Which of 19 frameworks might be most helpful right now
5. **Moments of opportunity**: When something important appears to be happening

**CRITICAL REQUIREMENT**: All clients must provide explicit, informed consent before MAIA is used in their treatment.

> - You can decline MAIA's use without affecting your treatment in any way
> - You can withdraw consent at any time
>
> Do you have questions? Are you comfortable with this?"

#### Three-Tier Consent Model

**TIER 1: Post-Session Analysis Only** (Minimum)
- Client consents to session recordings being analyzed after the fact
- No real-time MAIA presence during sessions
- Practitioner reviews MAIA reports between sessions
- Lowest technological footprint

**TIER 2: Real-Time Passive Monitoring** (Standard)
- Client consents to MAIA's real-time analysis during sessions
- Practitioner may glance at MAIA display during natural pauses
- MAIA provides gentle, non-intrusive cues
- Practitioner explains they may occasionally look at a device for supervision support

**TIER 3: Full Transparency** (Collaborative)
- Client is fully aware of MAIA's role and may even see some outputs
- Some practitioners share selected MAIA insights with clients when therapeutic
- Example: "MAIA noticed your coherence increased when you talked about X. Does that resonate?"
- Requires mature, psychologically sophisticated clients

#### Special Populations Requiring Enhanced Consent

**Minors (Under 18)**:
- Parent/guardian consent required in addition to minor's assent
- Age-appropriate explanation: "Your therapist uses a computer helper to be a better therapist"
- Additional privacy considerations for adolescents

**Court-Mandated/Forensic Clients**:
- Explicit disclosure that AI analysis is used
- Documentation that MAIA reports are **not** forensic evaluations
- Clear boundaries: MAIA's role is clinical support, not legal assessment

**Clients in Crisis/Acute States**:
- Consent obtained when client is stabilized and capable
- If real-time crisis intervention needed, consent discussion happens afterward
- Practitioners may disable MAIA during acute crisis if client cannot consent

**Culturally Diverse Clients**:
- Consent provided in client's primary language
- Cultural consultation if client's culture has specific views on AI/technology
- Extra care explaining "machine learning" to avoid mystification or alienation

#### Consent Withdrawal Process

Clients may withdraw consent at any time:

1. **Immediate cessation**: MAIA stops being used in client's treatment
2. **Data handling**:
   - Option A: Delete all recordings and MAIA analyses
   - Option B: Retain recordings per standard clinical records policy, but cease using MAIA
3. **No penalty**: Treatment continues without MAIA support
4. **Documentation**: Withdrawal documented in clinical record

#### Ongoing Consent Verification

- Consent re-verified at regular intervals (suggested: every 6 months or 20 sessions)
- Any change in MAIA's capabilities triggers new consent discussion
- Clients reminded periodically of their right to withdraw

- **Context window**: Models have limited memory (can't recall session from 6 months ago without retrieval system)

- **Quarterly updates**: New training data, bias corrections, capability improvements
- **Practitioner notification**: Changes to MAIA's capabilities trigger new consent discussions
- **Versioning**: Practitioners can choose to delay updates for stability
