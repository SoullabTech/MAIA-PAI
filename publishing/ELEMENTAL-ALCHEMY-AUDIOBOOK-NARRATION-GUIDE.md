## Pre-Production Setup

### 1. Recording Environment

**Optimal Setup:**
- Quiet room with soft surfaces (blankets, rugs reduce echo)
- Close door, turn off HVAC, silence notifications
- "Recording in session" sign on door
- Natural light preferred (affects energy)
- Stand or sit comfortably - posture matters for voice

**Sacred Space Prep:**
- Smudge the room before first session
- Set intention: "May this wisdom reach those who need it"
- Ground yourself before each recording session
- Water nearby (you'll need it)

### 2. Equipment Checklist

**Microphone:**
- USB mic recommended: Blue Yeti, Shure MV7, or Audio-Technica AT2020USB+
- Position 6-8 inches from mouth
- Slight angle (not directly in front) reduces plosives (p, b sounds)

**Headphones:**
- Closed-back headphones for monitoring
- Listen to your voice in real-time (helps with pacing)

**Computer:**
- MacBook or desktop with Descript installed
- External drive for backup (audiobooks are large files)

### 3. Vocal Preparation

**Before Each Session:**
- Warm up voice: hum, gentle scales, lip trills (5 minutes)
- Read aloud from any text to settle into voice
- Drink room-temperature water (cold constricts vocal cords)
- Avoid dairy before recording (causes mucus)
- Record in morning if possible (voice is freshest)

## Narration Guidelines by Section

### Front Matter

**Dedication, Acknowledgments, AI Statement**

- **Tone:** Warm, personal, gratitude-filled
- **Pacing:** Slower than body text (people skip these in print, make them worth hearing)
- **Energy:** Heart-centered, humble

**Example pacing:**
> "This book is dedicated... [pause] ...to the teachers who held space for my becoming. [pause] To the lineages that trusted me with their wisdom. [pause] And to you, dear reader, who chose to walk this spiral path."

### Introduction

**Tone:** Welcoming, authoritative, invitational

This sets the stage. You're opening ceremony.

- Slow down for key concepts (Spiralogic, elemental consciousness)
- Speed up for personal story (keep it flowing)
- Pause before and after major statements

**Key moment - 3:30am dream:**
Let emotion be present. This is the genesis moment. Your voice should reflect the awe.

### Part I: Fire Element

**Energy:** Passionate, igniting, dynamic

**Voice quality:**
- Slightly faster pacing (fire moves)
- More emphasis on action words
- Let excitement build in fire practices

**Reading Fire practices:**
- Slow down 20% when guiding breathwork or movement
- Pause between instructions
- Your voice should kindle the reader's inner fire

**Example:**
> "Place your hand on your solar plexus. [2-second pause] Feel the warmth there. [2-second pause] This is your inner fire. [2-second pause] Now, take a deep breath and imagine stoking that flame. [3-second pause]"

### Part II: Water Element

**Energy:** Flowing, emotional, adaptive

**Voice quality:**
- More fluid pacing (like waves)
- Softer consonants
- Let sentences flow into each other

**Reading Water practices:**
- Voice should be soothing, not drowsy
- Imagine speaking from water's perspective
- Allow natural rhythm (not forced smoothness)

**Emotional content:**
Water chapter may touch on grief, release, emotional healing. Let your voice honor this without dramatizing. Authenticity > performance.

### Part III: Earth Element

**Energy:** Grounded, stable, nurturing

**Voice quality:**
- Slower, more deliberate pacing
- Deeper register if natural
- Solid, dependable tone

**Reading Earth practices:**
- This is the SLOWEST section
- Let silence do the work
- Your voice should feel like bedrock

**Example:**
> "Ground down. [3-second pause] Feel your sit bones on the earth. [3-second pause] You are held. [3-second pause] You are supported. [4-second pause]"

### Part IV: Air Element

**Energy:** Clear, mental, expansive

**Voice quality:**
- Lighter tone (but not breathy)
- Crisp articulation
- Faster pacing for conceptual content

**Reading Air practices:**
- Mental clarity exercises: precise language
- Breathwork: guide the inhale/exhale timing with your voice
- Visualization: paint clear pictures with words

### Part V: Aether Element

**Energy:** Transcendent, mystical, integrative

**Voice quality:**
- Reverent but not hushed
- Spacious pacing (lots of silence)
- This is the HEART of the book

**Reading Aether practices:**
- Maximum presence required
- Speak from the center of your being
- Let transmission happen through you, not from you

**Key concept - the Spiralogic Process integration:**
This is where everything comes together. Your voice should reflect the synthesis. Not summarizing‚ÄîINTEGRATING.

### Conclusion & Integration

**Tone:** Completion with openness (spiral continues)

**Voice quality:**
- Satisfying but not final
- Acknowledge the journey
- Invite ongoing practice

**Avoid:**
- Sounding like "the end" (this is a living book)
- Rushing to finish (honor the closing)
- Performative emotion (stay authentic)

### Appendices & Resources

**Tone:** Practical, helpful, reference-oriented

- Faster pacing (this is reference material)
- Clear enunciation (people may be taking notes)
- Optional: Some listeners skip appendices, but yours should be worth hearing

## Technical Specifications

### Audiobook Industry Standards (ACX/Audible)

**Required specs:**
- Sample Rate: 44.1 kHz or 48 kHz
- Bit Depth: 16-bit or 24-bit
- Format: MP3 (192 kbps or higher) or M4B
- Channels: Mono or Stereo (Mono preferred for voice-only)
- Peak levels: -3 dB max
- RMS (average loudness): -18 dB to -23 dB
- Noise floor: -60 dB or lower
- No clipping, distortion, or background noise

**Descript handles most of this automatically**, but good to know.

### File Naming Convention

```
EA_Chapter_01_Title.wav (master files)
EA_Chapter_01_Title_FINAL.mp3 (export files)
```

**For Audible/ACX submission:**
```
01_Opening_Credits.mp3
02_Chapter_01.mp3
03_Chapter_02.mp3
...
99_Closing_Credits.mp3
```

### Backup Strategy

**After each recording session:**
1. Descript auto-saves to cloud
2. Export WAV files to external drive
3. Weekly backup entire Descript project folder

**You don't want to lose 10 hours of narration to a hard drive crash.**

## Post-Production Workflow

### Editing in Descript

**Descript is PERFECT for audiobooks because:**
- Auto-transcription lets you edit like a text document
- Select unwanted words/sounds in transcript ‚Üí Delete
- No waveform editing required
- Studio Sound feature removes background noise automatically

**Editing passes:**

**Pass 1: Content Editing**
- Remove false starts, verbal stumbles (um, uh, repeated words)
- Fix mispronunciations
- Ensure pacing feels right
- Add pauses where needed (just hit Enter in transcript)

**Pass 2: Sound Quality**
- Apply Studio Sound (removes background noise, evens levels)
- Check for mouth clicks (edit out or reduce)
- Ensure consistent volume across chapters

**Pass 3: Flow & Timing**
- Listen to full chapters start-to-finish
- Check transitions between sections
- Ensure chapter endings don't feel abrupt
- Verify opening/closing sequences are consistent

**Pass 4: Final QA**
- Export one chapter, listen on different devices (phone, car, headphones)
- Check for any technical issues
- Ensure metadata is correct

### Music & Sound Design (Optional)

**Minimalist approach recommended:**
- Opening ambient sound (3 seconds)
- Closing ambient sound (5 seconds)
- Optional: VERY subtle element-specific soundscapes during practices
  - Fire: gentle crackling
  - Water: soft flowing water
  - Earth: low drone/heartbeat
  - Air: light breeze
  - Aether: singing bowl or cosmic hum

**Important:** Soundscapes should be BARELY perceptible. Your voice is the primary instrument.

## Special Considerations

### Reading Exercises & Practices

**When you encounter a practice:**

1. **Announce it:** "Let's try this practice together. You may want to pause the recording and do this when you have 10 minutes of quiet time."

2. **Guide it:** If it's a short practice (2-3 minutes), guide it in real-time with appropriate pauses.

3. **Timing example for breathwork:**
   - "Breathe in for four counts. [4-second pause] Hold. [4-second pause] Breathe out for four counts. [4-second pause]"
   - Your voice becomes the timer

4. **Longer practices:** "This practice takes about 20 minutes. I'll guide you through the setup, then you can pause and complete it on your own, or continue listening as I walk you through it."

### Reading Case Studies & Stories

**When sharing personal stories or client examples:**

- Shift to storytelling mode (slightly more narrative)
- Maintain confidentiality (you've already anonymized in text)
- Let emotion be present without dramatizing
- Return to teaching tone after story completes

### Reading Technical/Academic Content

**When encountering psychological or shamanic terminology:**

- Don't rush through complex terms
- Slight emphasis on key concepts (but don't over-explain‚Äîtrust the text)
- If pronunciation is unclear, look it up before recording

**Example:**
- "Archetypal" (AR-kuh-TY-pul, not ar-kuh-TEP-i-kul)
- "Shamanic" (shuh-MAN-ik)
- "Aether" (EE-ther, like "ether")

### Maintaining Vocal Health

**During long recording sessions:**

- Take 5-minute break every 30 minutes
- Drink water (room temperature)
- Do gentle neck rolls
- If voice feels strained, STOP‚Äîdon't push through
- 2-hour max recording per session

**Recovery:**
- Vocal rest after recording (no loud talking)
- Hydrate well
- Avoid whispering (worse for vocal cords than speaking)
