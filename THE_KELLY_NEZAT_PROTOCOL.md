**Kelly Nezat (Clinical Psychologist)** observed a profound pattern working with female clients in their 40s:

### What You Noticed:
> "I started to do that informally with a few of my female clients in their 40s that I began to notice the pattern in their sessions and began to question them and notice where they were in their cycle. Lo and behold.. it was absolutely on point - the despair, hopelessness, depression, feeling of defeat etc was high in the **luteal phase** (phase before menstruation kicks off) and they were optimistic and hopeful and balanced in their perception of the **same problem 2 weeks later**, during the highest fertility phase (or their follicular phase)"

### The Amplification:
> "This instability only amplified during the **peri menopausal period** or in the very young women dealing with **PCOS** and trying to get to grips with their hormonal cycles and emotional realm."

### The Clinical Approach:
> "I know exactly what kind of support they are needing: **Luteal phase, pure presence, supportive, warm, listening, acknowledgement, reassurance that they're not mad or irrational or spiraling out**."

### What You DON'T Do:
> "I don't even really engage on anything that would resemble **real deep diving** at all during such sessions. It's really about **acknowledgment of how hard it is** to experience those emotions, those intrusive thoughts, that low mood, pessimism, anxiety of familiar themes haunting her, and that she really doesn't have something MORE she needs to do in that session, other than **take measures to take care of herself in a very loving way without judgment, analysis, searching, fixing** etc."

### The Center Concept:
> "It's more **reminding her of her own centre** and that this off-centre/negative experience, if a recurring theme noticed of course, may well bring **insights AFTER, but rarely something super accurate DURING**."
