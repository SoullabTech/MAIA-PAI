## How This Works in Practice

### Setup Option A: MAIA Listening (Passive)

**Physical setup**:
- You + Client in session (video or in-person)
- MAIA listening via mic/transcription
- MAIA analyzes in real-time
- MAIA silent UNLESS you or client invoke it

**Flow**:
1. You and client converse normally
2. MAIA transcribes and analyzes silently
3. YOU can check MAIA dashboard: "What's happening right now?"
4. CLIENT can ask: "MAIA, what do you see?"
5. MAIA responds to whoever asked

**Example**:

**CLIENT**: "I feel stuck in the same pattern again..."

**YOU**: [works with client, explores]

**YOU** (glancing at dashboard): *Hmm, MAIA shows coherence dropped to 0.22, Nigredo active*

**YOU** (to client): "I'm noticing something... would you be open to hearing what MAIA is picking up?"

**CLIENT**: "Yes"

**YOU** (clicks "Ask MAIA"): "MAIA, what do you see in this moment?"

**MAIA** (responds to both of you): "I witness a transition into Nigredo - dissolution phase. The pattern you call 'stuck' reads as Mortificatio operation: a necessary death process. The coherence score shows fragmentation (0.22), but this isn't pathology. Water and Shadow elements are active. This may be exactly where you need to be right now to allow what's dying to die."

**CLIENT**: "Oh... that actually helps. So I'm not failing?"

**YOU**: "What MAIA is pointing to is that this feeling of 'stuck' might actually be transformation in its darkest phase. Not something to fix, but to witness."

### Setup Option B: MAIA Active Participant (Co-Facilitator)

**Physical setup**:
- You + Client + MAIA (on screen or voice)
- MAIA has permission to interject
- MAIA offers observations without being asked
- More like a three-way conversation

**Flow**:
1. You and client explore an issue
2. MAIA notices significant pattern/moment
3. MAIA chimes in: "I'm noticing..."
4. You and client respond to MAIA's observation
5. Dialogue continues

**Example**:

**CLIENT**: "My therapist says I have abandonment issues, but I don't know..."

**YOU**: "What's your experience of that?"

**CLIENT**: "I just... I don't want to be clingy, you know?"

**MAIA** (interjects): "I notice the word 'clingy' carries a self-judgment. The coherence dropped when you said it. Can I reflect what I'm seeing?"

**CLIENT**: "Sure..."

**MAIA**: "The language pattern suggests an internal split: a part that needs connection (Water element) versus a part that judges that need (Shadow). IFS might frame this as a protector part preventing vulnerability."

**YOU**: "Does that land for you?"

**CLIENT**: "Yeah... the part that calls myself clingy is actually the one that keeps people away."

**YOU**: "So MAIA is pointing to the protector that's trying to prevent abandonment by... creating distance first."

**CLIENT**: "Exactly."
